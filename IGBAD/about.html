Our History

2021 - Foundation Established

IGBADI Foundation was founded on May 1, 2021 to represent the desires and development ambitions of community members in Kabarole District.

2021 - Initial Programs

Began caring for seven orphaned children (2 boys and 5 girls) from Harugongo and Kicwamba sub-counties.

2023 - Expanded Reach

Received additional requests for assistance from various communities in Kabarole district, totaling 14 children (5M,9F) and six families affected by domestic violence.

2024 - Official Registration

Formally registered as a Community Based Organisation (Reg. No. 2400) by the Kabarole District Local Government's Community Based Services Department.

2024 - MOU Signed

Established a 5-year Memorandum of Understanding with Kabarole District Local Government for collaborative development programs.

Our Mission & Vision

IGBADI Foundation exists to address the needs of underprivileged members in Kabarole District, with emphasis on promoting rights of the vulnerable and marginalized groups in the community.

We envision empowered communities that can effectively address their own social protection concerns, particularly for children, women, youth, and persons with disabilities.

Community-Led

Administered by local community members to ensure relevance and sustainability.

Compassionate

Addressing needs with empathy and understanding of local contexts.

Neutral

Politically and religiously neutral in all our operations.

Our Partners

Kabarole District Local Government

Our key partner in implementing development programs that prioritize care, social protection, environment protection and access to quality education for children and young people.

Through our Memorandum of Understanding, we collaborate on:

  • Family and system strengthening initiatives
  • Advocacy for children's/women rights
  • Capacity building for duty bearers
  • Environmental protection interventions
